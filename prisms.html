    *How to Calculate its Volume*

    Triangular Prism

    Objective: Given its formula for the area of the base, its variable values, and height, find the volume of a Triangular Prism.

    Given Values: h = 9; Ab = 1/2bhb; b = 4; hb = 5
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 'Ab' for '1/2bhb' into the formula, 'Abh'.
  • V = Abh
  • = (1/2bhb)h
    		•
    2 Substitute 'b' for '4', 'hb' for '5', and 'h' for '9' into the new formula.
  • V = (1/2bhb)h
  • = (1/2(4)(5))(9)
    		•
    3 Multiply '1/2' times '4'.
  • V = (1/2(4)(5))(9)
  • = ((2)(5))(9)
    		•
    4 Multiply '2' times '5'.
  • V = ((2)(5))(9)
  • = (10)(9)
    		•
    5 Multiply '10' times '9' to get the volume of the Triangular Prism.
  • V = (10)(9)
  • = 90 units3
    		•

    Objective: Given its formula for the area of the base, its variable values, and height, find the volume of a Cuboid.

    Given Values: h = 11; Ab = lw; l = 4; w = 6
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 'Ab' for 'lw' into the formula, 'Abh'.
  • V = Abh
  • = (lw)h
    		•
    2 Substitute 'l' for '4', 'w' for '5', and 'h' for '11' into the new formula.
  • V = (lw)h
  • = ((4)(6))(11)
    		•
    3 Multiply '4' times '6'.
  • V = ((4)(6))(11)
  • = (24)(11)
    		•
    4 Multiply '24' times '11' to get the volume of the Cuboid.
  • V = (24)(11)
  • = 264 units3

    Trapezoidal Prism

    Objective: Given its formula for the area of the base, its variable values, and height, find the volume of a Trapezoidal Prism.

    Given Values: h = 8; Ab = 1/2(b1+b2)hb; b1 = 7; b2 = 9; hb = 5
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 'Ab' for '1/2(b1+b2)hb' into the formula, 'Abh'.
  • V = Abh
  • = (1/2(b1+b2)hb)h
    		•
    2 Substitute 'b1' for '7', 'b2' for '9', 'hb' for '5', and 'h' for '8' into the new formula.
  • V = (1/2(b1+b2)hb)h
  • = (1/2((7)+(9))(5))(8)
    		•
    3 According to the 'Order of Operations', or 'PEMDAS', we must add up '7' and '9' together before we do anything else.
  • V = (1/2((7)+(9))(5))(8)
  • = (1/2(16)(5))(8)
    		•
    4 Multiply '1/2' times '16'.
  • V = (1/2(16)(5))(8)
  • = ((8)(5))(8)
    		•
    5 Multiply '8' times '5'.
  • V = ((8)(5))(8)
  • = (40)(8)
    		•
    6 Multiply '40' times '8'.
  • V = (40)(8)
  • = 320 units3
    		•

    Any Regular N-gonal Prism

    Objective: Given its formula for the area of the base, its variable values, and height, find the volume of a Trapezoidal Prism.

    Given Values: h = 5; Ab = 1/2ap; a = 7; p = 43
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 'Ab' for '1/2ap' into the formula, 'Abh'.
  • V = Abh
  • = (1/2(ap))(h)
    		•
    2 Substitute 'a' for '7', 'p' for '43', 'h' for '5' into the new formula.
  • A = (1/2(ap))(h)
  • = (1/2((7)(43)))(5)
    		•
    3 Multiply '7' times '43'.
  • A = (1/2((7)(43)))(5)
  • = (1/2(301))(5)
    		•
    4 Multiply '301' times '1/2'.
  • A = (1/2(301))(5)
  • = (150.5)(5)
    		•
    5 Multiply '150.5' times '5' to get the volume of the Regular N-gonal Prism.
  • A = (150.5)(5)
  • = 752.5 units3
