Vocabulary

  • Apothem:
    • The distance from the center of a regular polygon to the midpoint of a side.

  • Area:
    • The amount of space a 2D object takes up.

  • Base:
    • A line or surface on which a figure is standing on.

  • Circumference:
    • An enclosing boundary of a curved geometric figure.

  • Diameter:
    • A straight line passing from side to side through the center of a circle or sphere.

  • Height:
    • The distance from the base to the top of a geometric figure.

  • Length:
    • The distance between 2 points.

  • Perimeter:
    • A continuous line forming the boundary of a polygon.

  • N-gon:
    • A polygon with N sides.

  • Order of Operations (PEMDAS):
    • A collection of mathematical steps that define which steps to perform first in order to evaluate a given mathematical expression. The following must be followed in order:
    1. P - Parentheses
    2. E - Exponents
    3. M - Multiplication
    4. D - Division
    5. A - Addition
    6. S - Subtraction


  • Quadrilateral:
    • A 4-sided polygon.

  • Radius:
    • The distance between the circumference to the center of circle.

  • Volume:
    • The amount of space a 3D object takes up.

  • Width:
    • The distance between 2 points.
