    *How to Calculate its Area*

    Square

    Objective: Given its side length, find the area of a Square.

    Given Value: s = 4
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 's' for '4' into the formula, 's2'.
  • A = s2
  • = (4)2
    		•
    2 Square '4' to get the area of the Square.
  • A = (4)2
  • = 16 units2
    		•

    Rectangle

    Objective: Given its length and width, find the area of a Rectangle.

    Given Values: l = 13; w = 7
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 'l' for '13' and 'w' for '7' into the formula, 'lw'.
  • A = lw
  • = (13)(7)
    		•
    2 Multiply '13' times '7' to get the area of the Rectangle.
  • A = (13)(7)
  • = 91 units2
    		•

    Parallelogram

    Given Values: b = 7; h = 6
    Step Explanation Work

  • A = bh

    		•

    Trapezoid

    Objective: Given base1, base2, and its height, find the area of a Trapezoid.

    Given Values: b1 = 5; b2 = 7; h = 4
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 'b1' for '5', 'b2' for '7', and 'h' for '4' into the formula, '1/2(b1+b2)h'.
  • A = 1/2(b1+b2)h
  • = 1/2((5)+(7))(4)
    		•
    2 According to the 'Order of Operations', or 'PEMDAS', we must add up '5' and '7' together before we do anything else.
  • A = 1/2((5)+(7))(4)
  • = 1/2(12)(4)
    		•
    3 Multiply '1/2' times '12'.
  • A = 1/2(12)(4)
  • = (6)(4)
    		•
    4 Multiply '6' times '4' to get the area of the Trapezoid.
  • A = (6)(4)
  • = 24 units2
