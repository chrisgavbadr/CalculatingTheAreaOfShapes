    *How to Calculate its Volume*

    Sphere

    Objective: Given its radius, find the volume of a Sphere.

    Given Values: r = 3
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 'r' for '3' into the formula, '4/3πr3'.
  • V = 4/3πr3
  • = 4/3π(3)3
    		•
    2 According to the 'Order of Operations', or 'PEMDAS', cube the radius BEFORE you multiply the radius to π. So we're going to cube '3'.
  • V = 4/3π(3)3
  • = 4/3π(27)
    		•
    3 Multiply '4/3' times '27'.
  • V = 4/3π(27)
  • = π(36)
    		•
    4 Multiply '4/3' times 'π' to get the volume of the Sphere.
  • V = π(36)
  • ≈ 113.1 units3
    		•

    Cylinder

    Objective: Given its radius and height, find the volume of a Cylinder.

    Given Values: r = 5; h = 8
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 'r' for '5' and 'h' for '8' into the formula, 'πr2h'.
  • V = πr2h
  • = π(5)2(8)
    		•
    2 According to the 'Order of Operations', or 'PEMDAS', square the radius BEFORE you do anything else. So we're going to square '5'.
  • V = π(5)2(8)
  • = π(25)(8)
    		•
    3 Multiply '25' times '8'.
  • V = π(25)(8)
  • = π(200)
    		•
    4 Multiply '200' by 'π' to get the volume of the Cylinder.
  • V = π(200)
  • ≈ 628.32 units3
    		•

    Cone

    Objective: Given its radius and height, find the volume of a Cone.

    Given Values: r = 7; h = 5
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 'r' for '7' and 'h' for '5' into the formula, 'πr2h/3'.
  • V = πr2h/3
  • = π(7)2(5)/3
    		•
    2 According to the 'Order of Operations', or 'PEMDAS', square the radius BEFORE you do anything else. So we're going to square '7'.
  • V = π(7)2(5)
  • = π(49)(5)/3
    		•
    3 Multiply '49' times '5'.
  • V = π(49)(5)/3
  • = π(245)/3
    		•
    4 Multiply '245/3' by 'π' to get the volume of the Cone.
  • V = π(245)/3
  • ≈ 256.56 units3
