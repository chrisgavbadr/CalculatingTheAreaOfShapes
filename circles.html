    *How to Calculate its Area*

    Circle

    Objective: Given its radius or diameter, find the area of a circle.

    Case 1 | Given Value: r = 5
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Substitute 'r' for '5' into the formula, 'πr2'.
  • A = πr2
  • = π(5)2
    		•
    2 According to the 'Order of Operations', or 'PEMDAS', square the radius BEFORE you multiply the radius to π. So we're going to square '5'.
  • A = π(5)2
  • = π(25)
    		•
    3 Multiply '25' times 'π' to get the area of the Circle.
  • A = π(25)
  • ≈ 78.54 units2
    		•
    Case 2 | Given Value: d = 6
    Step Explanation Work
    1 Considering that the diameter is twice the radius, that means that we'll have to divide the diameter by 2 (r = d/2) in order to get the radius so we can substitute and calculate the area. So we're going to substitute 'r' for 'd/2' to get a new formula for this type of case.
  • A = πr2
  • = π(d/2)2
    		•
    2 Substitute 'd' for '6' into the new formula, 'π(d/2)2'.
  • A = π(d/2)2
  • = π((6)/2)2
    		•
    3 Divide '6' by '2', so the current value of the number is now the radius because remember, the radius is half of the diameter.
  • A = π((6)/2)2
  • = π(3)2
    		•
    4 According to the 'Order of Operations', or 'PEMDAS', square the radius BEFORE you multiply the radius to π. So we're going to square '3'.
  • A = π(3)2
  • = π(9)
    		•
    5 Multiply '3' times 'π' to get the area of the Circle.
  • A = π(9)
  • ≈ 28.27 units2
